Sentence writing task for Group 3

Instructions:

This task will involve reading a line from an unfamiliar text and writing three sentences that relate to it. Using only this text and what you know about the world:

  • Write one sentence that is deﬁnitely correct about the situation or event in the line.
  • Write one sentence that might be correct about the situation or event in the line.
  • Write one sentence that is deﬁnitely incorrect about the situation or event in the line.

As an added constraint, there are some words that you cannot use in your statement:

  • The deﬁnitely correct sentence cannot use the following words: there, can, may, might, some, people
  • The deﬁnitely maybe sentence cannot use the following words: many, most, some, other, will, would, often
  • The deﬁnitely incorrect sentence cannot use the following words: no, never, not/n’t, only, always, all, any

One example is provided for you below. If you would like to see additional examples or answers to common questions about this task, please refer to our FAQ page here.

Text: Well aware of the island's burgeoning wealth and repository of supplies, the French pirate Bertrand de Montluc sailed into Funchal harbor with his 11-galleon armada and 1,300 men.

  • Deﬁnitely correct statement: Bertrand de Montluc arrived at the island with a great force of men.
  • Maybe correct statement: Montluc plundered and looted the island, killing several of its residents.
  • Definitely incorrect statement: The pirate Bertrand de Montluc avoided the wealthy island owing to its heavily armed garrison.

Here's some additional rules of thumb to keep in mind when writing your statements (more details on each of these can be found in the FAQ document):

  • Make sure the sentence is plausible outside the context of this task, especially in the definitely incorrect sentences
  • Similarly, your definitely correct statement shouldn’t be so vague that it could be true given any text at all
  • Your statement must relate to the event that the text describes in some way

Writing "clever" statements

We're additionally on the lookout for statements that are "clever" or "tricky". Use the textbox at the bottom to let us know where you've tried to do this, and we can assign additional bonuses if you're consistently applying a "clever" strategy. Here are some examples of what we mean by "clever" statements (more details on each of these can be found in the FAQ document):

  • Use an idiomatic or metaphorical expression
  • Reverse the order that phrases appear in
  • Leave out the subject or object of the verb (e.g., through passivization. The statement should still be grammatically correct)
  • Make reasonable inferences
  • Rely on common sense knowledge
  • Write something humorous
